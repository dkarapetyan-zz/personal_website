8.Nc3 0-0 9.Bd3 b6 Black prepares to trade the light-squared bishops, that should promise him an equal position.

10.0-0 Bxc3 11.bxc3 Ba6 12.Bxa6 Nxa6 13.Qa4 attacking the knight, and, at the same time, preparing to carry out c3-c4. [13.Qe2 Qb7 14.c4 Rac8 ---> 1/2-1/2 Sermek,D-Cvitan,O/Solin/Spilt 2002/CBM 93/[Kostakiev,D] (67)]

13...Qb7 14.c4 Rac8 Black's plan is very simple: he puts his rooks on the "c" and "d" files to attack white's hanging pawns. In opposite, white is willing to carry out the breakthrough d4-d5 in the centre. Another plan for white is to push forward his "a" pawn in order to create a weakness in the black camp on the queenside.

15.Rac1N This move was played for the first time. White didn't have to define the position of his queenside rook immediately. More flexible was to keep it on a1, saving the both options of Ra1-b1 and Ra1-c1. In the preceding games white tried the following continuations: [15.Qb3 Nb8 16.a4 Qc7 17.Rfc1 Nc6 18.Bg5 Na5= 0-1 Yudovich,M-Kotov,A/Moscow 1942/HCL (40); 15.Rfc1 Nb8 16.Rab1 Nbd7 17.h3 Rc6 18.Ne5 Nxe5 19.dxe5 Nd7 20.c5 Rfc8 21.cxb6 Rxc1+= 0-1 Manca,F-Del Rio Angelis,S/Montecatini Terme 1999/EXT 2000 (39); 15.Rab1 Nb8 16.Ne5 Nc6 17.f4 Rfd8 18.c5 Nd5=/+ 0-1 Versmissen,J-Muhren,B/SFch-NED w, Leeuwarden NED (1.2) 2004 (28)]
