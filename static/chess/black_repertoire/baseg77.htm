9...Nxd7 10.0-0 e6 '=' Psakhis,L. Stuart thought for a long time here but couldn't find any way to make use of his slight lead in development. For my part, I was mulling over a conversation I once had with GM Keith Arkell who claimed that these structures (Which he used to get from the line 1 d4 Nf6 2 Nf3 g6 3 b4!? after a subsequent e5 and Black taking with the knight) favour the side with the e-pawn in the long term. This is basically because when you strip the position to its bare essentials, Black can advance his queenside pawns to weaken white's structure, while white cannot readily do the same thing on the kingside for fear of weakening his own king. Even in an ending this factor is relevant because Black's king will normally be closer to his own weakness, even if White manages to create one. This is rather abstract of course, but in such a boring position one needs to think of something.
